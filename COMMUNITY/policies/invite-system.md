# Invite System

**TheMicroStartup** uses an **invite-only model** for the physical space while maintaining **open-source** access to digital resources.

---

## Philosophy

- **Digital: Open** - Anyone can contribute to code, docs, and ideas
- **Physical: Vetted** - The retreat space is invite-only to ensure safety and alignment
- **Community: Growing** - Invites are earned through contribution and alignment

---

## Invitation Criteria

### For Physical Space Access

Potential members should demonstrate:

1. **Alignment with Values**
   - Commitment to self-sustainability
   - Respect for community principles
   - Maker mindset (builds things, not just consumes)

2. **Meaningful Contribution**
   - Active contributions to TheMicroStartup projects
   - Helpful participation in community discussions
   - Sharing knowledge or resources

3. **Positive Community Impact**
   - Supportive of other members
   - Constructive feedback and communication
   - Collaborative spirit

4. **Safety & Respect**
   - Adherence to Code of Conduct
   - Respect for space and resources
   - Responsible behavior

### Not Required (But Valued)

- Specific skills or expertise
- Previous experience
- Financial contribution
- Age or background

---

## Invitation Process

### Step 1: Contribution Phase

1. **Start Contributing**
   - Submit ideas in `IDEAS/`
   - Join or start projects in `PROJECTS/`
   - Improve documentation
   - Participate in discussions

2. **Build Reputation**
   - Consistent positive contributions
   - Helpful community participation
   - Alignment with values

### Step 2: Nomination Phase

1. **Be Noticed**
   - Maintainers or moderators notice your contributions
   - Existing members nominate you
   - You demonstrate readiness

2. **Nomination Review**
   - Review of contributions
   - Community feedback
   - Value alignment check

### Step 3: Invitation Phase

1. **Receive Invitation**
   - Formal invitation extended
   - Terms and expectations shared
   - Onboarding process begins

2. **Onboarding**
   - Orientation session
   - Space guidelines review
   - Community introduction

## Invitation Responsibilities

### For Inviters

- **Vouch for invitee** - You recommend them based on:
  - Knowledge of their work
  - Confidence in their alignment
  - Trust in their behavior

- **Mentorship** - Help onboard and guide:
  - Introduce to community
  - Explain norms and processes
  - Support initial contributions

### For Invitees

- **Respect the opportunity** - Honor the trust placed in you
- **Follow guidelines** - Adhere to Code of Conduct and space rules
- **Contribute meaningfully** - Continue to add value
- **Pay it forward** - Support future members when ready

---

## Waiting Period

After receiving invitation, there's typically:

- **1-2 weeks** - Onboarding and orientation
- **1 month** - Probationary period (varies)
- **Ongoing** - Community participation expected

---

## Removing Invitations

Invitations can be revoked if:

- Code of Conduct violations
- Misuse of space or resources
- Disrespectful behavior
- Consistent misalignment with values

Process:
1. Warning and discussion
2. Opportunity to correct
3. Review by moderators
4. Final decision

### Partners & Collaborators

- Organizations or individuals with complementary missions
- Formal partnership agreements
- Defined collaboration terms

### Speakers & Workshop Leaders

- Short-term invitations for events

- Can lead to longer-term relationships

### Researchers & Academics

- Data sharing agreements

## How to Get Invited

**The best way to get invited is to contribute:**

1. **Start Small**
   - Submit an idea
   - Improve documentation
   - Answer questions in discussions

2. **Be Consistent**
   - Regular contributions show commitment
   - Quality over quantity
   - Helpful participation matters

3. **Show Alignment**
   - Demonstrate understanding of values
   - Support sustainability goals
   - Build, don't just consume

4. **Engage Authentically**
   - Be yourself
   - Share your unique perspective
   - Support others

5. **Be Patient**
   - Trust takes time
   - Contribution history matters
   - Community relationships develop organically
