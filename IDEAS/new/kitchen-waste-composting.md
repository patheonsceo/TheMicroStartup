## Overview

Set up a simple composting system to turn kitchen food waste into fertilizer. This reduces waste, creates useful compost for the garden, and cuts down on garbage disposal costs.

---

## Problem Statement

Kitchen waste ends up in trash. This costs money to dispose of and creates landfill waste. Instead, we can turn it into compost for the garden.

---

## Proposed Solution

Use 2-3 large compost bins near the kitchen. Collect vegetable peels, fruit scraps, and other food waste. Mix with dry leaves or sawdust. Turn it regularly. In 2-3 months, we'll have compost ready for the garden.

- Needs regular attention (weekly mixing)
- Can attract insects if not managed properly
- Takes time to make compost (2-3 months)
- Needs education on what can and can't be composted
